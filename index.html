About

I'm a PhD candidate at Zhejiang University's Ocean Sensing & Networking Institute. My work sits at the intersection of adaptive signal processing, noise cancellation, and real-time embedded systems — applied to extreme communication environments from kilometers-deep drilling mud to coastal underwater channels.

Currently exploring audio algorithm development. I've worked across MATLAB, Python, Verilog/FPGA, and embedded Linux/STM32 stacks, and I care about algorithms that survive contact with the real world.

Research & Projects

2023–2026 · Core Member

High-Speed Downhole Information Transmission

SINOPEC "Jiebai Guashuai" Project

Multi-source pump noise model in MATLAB; Kalman + LMS/RLS adaptive filtering achieving >99% noise reconstruction accuracy and 15dB+ SNR improvement. Led real-time software architecture in App Designer — 1s display latency, 90%+ efficiency gain.

High-Speed Underwater Acoustic Communication

National Science Fund for Distinguished Young Scholars

Applied STFT, notch filtering, and MVDR beamforming to suppress thruster noise — 10dB+ SNR gain, BER below 1e-5. Designed SNR-adaptive frame synchronization in Verilog/FPGA; zero frame-miss in 5km Zhoushan sea trial.

Linux + STM32 embedded system; OpenCV image processing and Python OCR. PID motor control for underwater line-following, obstacle avoidance, and target recognition — National 1st Prize.
